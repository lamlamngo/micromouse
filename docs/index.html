Project Influence

Influenced by the Cherno Alpha Jaeger design by Russia in Guillermo Del Toro's seminal Pacific Rim in 2013. Russian like designs call for a design that is rugged, massive in size and packs a lot of power. Powered by a 11.1V battery with 1500mAh capacity and two 200 Steps/Rev and 3.6 A/phase stepper motors on a 3D printed chassis, I entered the 2018 IEEE Robotics Olympiad held at Brown University with my Micromouse version of Cherno Alpha

Implementation

The chasis, designed by Anthony Xu (Union College), was 3D printed.





Wall sensing are povided through usual IR sensors. One at the front and two on the sides. Motors are two stepper motors, which are prices and thus there was no need for a PID controller. Two side sensors are also used to realign the robot as it goes.

Two motors are controlled by two stepper Sparkfun motor drivers and received signals from a Arduino UNO microcontroller. They are first wired directly, and soldered onto a protoboard for a cleaner version.

Voice-controlled using Google Assistant

Google AIY Voice Kit voice 1 is used for this project. It is powered by a Raspberry Pi 3. My code uses Google Cloud Speech and Google Assistant python API. It can behave as an usual Google Assistant with hot word detection: OK Google or Hey Google.

My Bluetooth Module enables the robot to be connected remotely. Locals commands are added so that the Google Assistant can find nearby Bluetooth devices and connect to the robot. It can move the robot forward, turn left, right, 180 and reset to origin. It can also set the robot in autonomous mode, which will enable floodfill algorithm.
